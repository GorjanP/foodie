Preheat an oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).Watch Now    
Cut a 12x14-inch sheet of aluminum foil. Fold it in half lengthwise, then in half again widthwise.Watch Now    
Wrap the foil partially around a spice jar lengthwise, bringing foil up around the jar and crushing the foil around the ends of the jar to make a boat-shaped baking form about 4 inches long, 2 1/2 inches tall, and 1 1/2 inch wide. Repeat with 15 more pieces of foil to make 16 boat-shaped baking forms. Shape the bottoms of the forms to be slightly flat so forms can stand up.Watch Now    
Spray the forms with cooking spray, and place onto a baking sheet.Watch Now    
Mix together 1 cup water, eggs, and vegetable oil in a bowl with an electric mixer set on low speed.Watch Now    
Beat in yellow cake mix on medium speed for 2 minutes.Watch Now    
Beat in the vanilla pudding mix until smooth, about 3 minutes more.Watch Now    
Divide the batter evenly into the prepared aluminum foil baking forms, filling each form about 1/2 full.Watch Now    
Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 11 to 15 minutes. Cool in the forms for 10 minutes before removing to cool completely on a wire rack.Watch Now    
Beat together the butter, shortening, vanilla extract, and salt in a bowl with an electric mixer until smooth, about 3 minutes.Watch Now    
Beat in the confectioners' sugar on low speed, gradually increasing speed to high until frosting is smooth and fluffy.Watch Now    
Mix in the marshmallow fluff, beating on high speed until light and airy.Watch Now    
Spoon frosting into a pastry bag equipped with a round pastry tip.Watch Now    
Remove the cakes from the baking forms.    
With a sharp knife, cut a 2-inch slit lengthwise on the flat bottom side of a cake.Watch Now    
Pipe a generous amount of the frosting into the slit, holding the shape of the cake with your hand to prevent splitting. Repeat with the remaining cakes.Watch Now    
For the coconut-raspberry variation: Bring 2 cups water and raspberry gelatin mix to a boil in a saucepan until the gelatin dissolves, about 5 minutes.    
Pour mixture into a shallow dish and refrigerate until thickened but not set, about 15 minutes.    
Place coconut flakes in a food processor. Pulse until flakes are finely chopped.    
Transfer the coconut to a shallow dish and stir in enough cornstarch to achieve a loose texture.    
Dip a filled cake in the thickened gelatin, turning to coat all sides; roll the cake in the coconut mixture. Repeat with remaining cakes.    
Arrange the coconut-coated cakes on a wire rack until gelatin and coconut are set, about 2 hours.
